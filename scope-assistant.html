The SCOPE Framework

S

Situation Assessment

Get a clear understanding of where you really are and what problems exist

C

Context Analysis

Identify factors that actually influence outcomes. Market conditions, competitive landscape

O

Opportunity Identification

Discover leverage points where focused effort creates maximum impact

P

Path Development

Chart a systematic approach to implementation. Clear steps, realistic timelines

E

Evaluation Framework

Measure progress and adjust based on results. Track the right metrics

See SCOPE in Action

What it does: Maps your current reality without the optimism bias that derails most strategic planning.

Example: A mid-market consulting firm discovered their "strong pipeline" was actually 80% unqualified leads. Situation Assessment revealed the real conversion bottleneck before they wasted budget on lead generation.

What it does: Identifies the external factors that will help or hinder your strategic moves.

Example: A SaaS company planning expansion learned their target market was consolidating. Context Analysis revealed three acquisition targets that would double their addressable market at lower cost than organic growth.

What it does: Surfaces the high-leverage moves that create disproportionate results.

Example: A professional services firm found that their fastest-growing clients all shared one characteristic: they had recently hired a new CTO. This insight shifted their entire business development strategy to focus on executive transitions.

What it does: Creates actionable implementation roadmaps with realistic timelines and dependencies.

Example: An agency identified market repositioning as their best opportunity, but Path Development revealed they needed to upgrade their portfolio first. The systematic approach prevented a premature launch that would have undermined credibility.

What it does: Establishes metrics that actually predict success, not vanity metrics that feel good.

Example: A consulting practice stopped measuring "proposals sent" and started tracking "qualified conversations with decision-makers." Revenue increased 40% in six months by focusing on the metric that actually mattered.
